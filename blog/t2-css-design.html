CSS: Margin, Border, Padding

10-18-14 by Joseph Won

CSS allows us to control the size, shape, placement, and much more of anything in our HTML. Essentially, we can control the visual and design elements of a website. Three important attributes to consider are margin, border, and padding. What do they do? They all seem to be terms that surrounds something, and if that's what you thought, you've already got some grasp of what it can do.

See the blue area in the middle? That's where the content goes, whether it is colored space, text, or even an image. Let's take one step outside of it, here we venture into padding. If we have two blocks of content (let's call each of these a block), padding is the space that surrounds it, but we can't see this space. One more important thing about padding is that it affects our border.
